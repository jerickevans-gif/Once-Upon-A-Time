December 2025

Once Upon A Time

Written by Sydney Phelps · Published Jan 18, 2026 · 8 min read

This December marked the close of our most ambitious year yet — across nine programs, we welcomed more than 100 children into the studio for arts education that meets them where they are.

The end of year showcase brought 200+ family members into Studio Room A for a one-night-only celebration of the work our students put in over the season. From beginner ballet to advanced jazz, every track had a moment to shine.

Community Garden

Our gardening track expanded this year to include weekly trips to the Queens Botanical Garden. Students planted, harvested, and learned to care for native species — bringing home cherry tomatoes by the bagful in late summer.

“The classes turned my Saturday mornings into the best part of my week.” — Jane, 16

Volunteer Spotlight

Lena Stuart joined as a volunteer in October and has already chaperoned three field trips, captured photo coverage for two showcases, and helped Mia Kim run the Jazz Dance warm-ups every Thursday.

Lena's day job is at the Pepsi-Cola plant in Long Island City, but her evenings have been a steady source of generosity for our after-school programs.
